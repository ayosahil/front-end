MERCURY

Mercury, the innermost planet of the solar system and the eighth in size and mass. Its closeness to the Sun and its smallness make it the most elusive of the planets visible to the unaided eye. Because its rising or setting is always within about two hours of the Sun’s, it is never observable when the sky is fully dark. Mercury is designated by the symbol ☿. Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun, at a distance of 57.91 million kilometers / 35.98 miles or 0.4 AU away. It takes sunlight 3.2 minutes to travel from the Sun to Mercury.

JUPITER

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system and is known for its prominent Great Red Spot. It is the fifth planet from the Sun and has a strong magnetic field. Jupiter is a gas giant with a mass more than 300 times that of Earth and is made up mostly of hydrogen and helium.

EARTH

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known place in the universe to harbor life. It has a diverse climate and is home to millions of species, including humans. Earth's atmosphere is rich in nitrogen and oxygen, which are essential for life as we know it.
